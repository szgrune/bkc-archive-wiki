# AI, Labor & the Economy

Two intertwined anxieties dominate: **jobs** and the **bubble**. On labor, the archive
tracks AI eating entry-level work, coders' jobs degraded toward "warehouse work,"
"workslop" destroying productivity, and AI workers warning their own families away
from the tools. On macro-economics, recurring pieces frame an **AI bubble** and warn
the public may end up "bailing out" the AI business, with venture capital
"cannibalizing the economy." Tim Wu's *Age of Extraction* supplies the synthesizing
frame — concentrated platform power extracting value — and connects this topic to
[[platform-power-and-antitrust]]. The hidden-labor angle (African data workers, Kenyan
"digital dirty work") links to [[digital-colonialism-and-global-south]].
