# Facial Recognition, Biometrics & Deepfakes

Two converging threads about **machine perception of the body**. The biometrics thread
starts in the Global South — India's Aadhaar, Pakistan's biometric ID "stripping
citizenship" — and arrives at municipal **facial-recognition bans** (Portland OR and
ME) and the EU AI Act's fight over real-time recognition. The deepfakes thread begins
as an epistemics worry ("worry about why people fall for them," "how deepfakes scramble
our sense of true and false") and hardens, by the mid-2020s, into concrete harm:
**non-consensual intimate imagery**, real-time deepfake scam operations, and Germany
weighing deepfake-porn penalties. By 2025 the two merge with chatbots into a general
"erosion of shared reality." The consistent argument is that the harm is less the
technology's realism than the **social and legal vacuum** it exploits. Overlaps
[[privacy-and-surveillance]] and the enforcement angle in
[[surveillance-and-immigration-tech]].
