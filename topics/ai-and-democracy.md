# AI & Democracy

A two-sided debate runs through the archive: AI as **threat to** democracy
(disinformation, an "AI president," eroded electoral trust, technofeudalism) and AI
as **tool for** democracy (deliberative democracy frameworks, civic tech, AI used to
"strengthen democracies worldwide"). 2025 is a transition year — much of the early
material reflects on how AI shaped the 2024 global elections, while late-year items
look ahead to the **2026 US midterms** and to persuasion experiments pairing humans
with AI. BKC-adjacent voices (Bruce Schneier and Nathan Sanders) are prominent on the
constructive side; TechPolicy.Press supplies much of the critical, power-focused
analysis.
