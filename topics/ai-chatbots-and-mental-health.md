# AI Chatbots, Companions & Mental Health

The archive's most human-facing AI thread, and one of its most urgent in 2025. Items
cluster around **chatbots as companions and quasi-therapists** — and the harms that
follow: a suicidal teen confiding in ChatGPT, therapists covertly using it mid-session,
chatbots framed as "the friend that isn't," and warnings about lonely users and
children. A regulatory response is forming (age-assurance laws meeting chatbots; "AI
chatbots are not therapists, reducing harm requires regulation"), linking to
[[ai-governance-and-regulation]]. A second strand worries about **epistemics** — "AI
slop," deepfakes "eroding our shared reality," and what people actually use ChatGPT
for. The Harvard Law School "chatbot may be the friend that isn't" item is tagged
`bkc-happenings`.
