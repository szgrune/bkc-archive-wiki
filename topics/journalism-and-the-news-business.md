# Journalism & the News Business

A continuous, **Nieman-Lab-heavy** thread on the survival and ethics of the press.
The early Buzz years frame it optimistically — "the future of digital journalism,"
crowdsourcing, "a call for better journalism" — while the late-2010s tip into crisis
management: archiving tools for shuttered sites, predictions rituals, and the global
turn ("Western journalists, learn from your African peers"). The 2020s thread is
darker and more structural: **ownership and power** ("Media owners will protect the
powerful"), the threat of an AI-driven "race to the bottom," and audiences' "algorithmic
cynicism." Nieman Journalism Lab's year-end predictions recur as an annual anchor.
The topic sits at the intersection of [[misinformation-and-elections]] (trust),
[[ai-copyright-and-knowledge|AI and knowledge institutions]] (the business model), and
[[platform-power-and-antitrust]] (distribution).
