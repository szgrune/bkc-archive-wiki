# Misinformation, Disinformation & Elections

A thread that surges around election cycles and then matures into self-critique. The
2016–2020 peak frames it as a **platform-and-bots** problem (Russian influence,
"fake news," fact-checking infrastructure); by the early 2020s the archive pivots to
arguing that **people, not technology, are the binding constraint** ("People Are
Disinformation's Biggest Problem, Not AI") and that the panic itself was overstated —
the 2024 "apocalypse that wasn't," where the feared deepfake election largely failed
to materialize. By 2025 the dominant note is **post-mortem**: "We Failed the
Misinformation Fight. Now What?" — even as AI-driven influence operations resurface
in Global-South elections. The recurring tension is between treating misinformation as
a content-moderation problem and treating it as a symptom of broken trust and
[[journalism-and-the-news-business|journalism]].
