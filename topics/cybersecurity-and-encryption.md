# Cybersecurity, Encryption & Spyware

Security in the archive is consistently framed as a **human-rights and power** story,
not a purely technical one. The Buzz years cover the crypto-policy staples — NSA
surveillance, Apple's iPhone encryption fight, "why the NSA should keep our data." From
2017 the dominant thread is **mercenary spyware**, carried heavily by Citizen Lab
reporting: Cyberbit targeting Ethiopian dissidents, NSO/Pegasus against Mexican
journalists, "The Autocrat in Your iPhone." State-sponsored intrusion (the FireEye /
SolarWinds compromise) and a persistent "we need to be more imaginative about
cybersecurity" reform argument round out the late 2010s–early 2020s. By 2025–2026 the
frame fuses with AI on both sides — AI as attacker and defender, and the "global
cybersecurity gap" that frontier labs both widen and claim to close. Tightly coupled to
[[privacy-and-surveillance]] and, via spyware's export to authoritarian states,
[[digital-colonialism-and-global-south]].
