# Privacy, Data & Surveillance

The longest-running thread in the archive, traceable from the Buzz era to the present.
Its center of gravity **shifts decade to decade**: in the early 2010s it is **state
surveillance and big data** (the NSA/Snowden moment, "the privacy problems of big
data"); by the late 2010s it is **corporate data extraction** (the census, Google's
China project, location-data brokers); and in the 2020s it fuses with AI and the
border — a **surveillance ecosystem** in which commercial data brokers, spyware
vendors, and AI-driven border tech are read as one continuous infrastructure rather
than discrete harms. A recurring analytic move (Woodrow Hartzog, the LPE Project) is
to reject the consent-and-notice framing of privacy as "the wrong question" and to
recast it as a problem of power and structural exposure. This is the broad cross-year
topic; for the 2025 ICE/immigration-specific cluster see
[[surveillance-and-immigration-tech]].
