# Algorithms & Algorithmic Accountability

The conceptual backbone connecting the archive's pre-AI and AI eras. It begins as
unease about **algorithmic curation** (whether Twitter should reorder the timeline,
Facebook's "oracular algorithm") and crystallizes around a 2016 demand to **"hold
algorithms accountable."** The late-2010s frame is **fairness and bias** — risk
assessments in the justice system, "an algorithm in the loop," search-result
manipulation, "biased algorithms are easier to fix than biased people." Then comes a
deliberate widening: the **"second wave of algorithmic accountability"** (2022) argues
that auditing individual systems for bias misses the point, and the archive starts
**"thinking of algorithms as institutions"** that govern — coming for democracy, shaping
what young audiences will even trust ("algorithmic cynicism," "after the feed"). The
methodological substrate beneath [[content-moderation-and-speech]],
[[misinformation-and-elections]], and [[ai-governance-and-regulation]].
