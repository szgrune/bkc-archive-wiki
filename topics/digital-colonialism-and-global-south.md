# Digital Colonialism & the Global South

A distinctive emphasis of this archive — far heavier on **non-Western perspectives**
than a typical US tech-policy feed, reflecting BKC's global network. The core claim,
sharpened by "The AI Raj: how tech giants are recolonizing power," is that the AI
economy reproduces colonial relations: dependence on foreign (US/Chinese) AI, hidden
African data labor, extraction of land and minerals, and "digital colonialism" in
health messaging. A counter-current documents **Global South agency** — Latin
America's "quiet leadership," India's talent looking beyond the US, sovereign-tech and
digital-commons initiatives, and internet fragmentation as resistance as well as
threat. Rest of World is the workhorse source here; Mailyn Fidler recurs on
fragmentation.
