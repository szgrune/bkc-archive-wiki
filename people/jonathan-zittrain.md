# Jonathan Zittrain

Harvard Law professor and **co-founder / faculty director of the Berkman Klein
Center** — the archive's central figure, recurring from the Buzz years to the present.
His throughline is a distinctive set of conceptual frames that the field keeps adopting:
**"generativity"** (the open system's capacity for unanticipated innovation, and its
fragility), the **"right to be forgotten"** and intermediary law, **"intellectual debt"**
(relying on AI answers we can't explain), the **"Great Deplatforming"**, and most
recently **AI agents and trust**. He moves fluidly between [[content-moderation-and-speech]],
[[algorithmic-accountability]], and [[ai-safety-and-agents]], usually supplying the
governance-and-trust vocabulary the debate then runs on.
