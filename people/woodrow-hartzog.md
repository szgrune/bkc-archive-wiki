# Woodrow Hartzog

A privacy-law scholar (Boston University School of Law) whose work supplies the
archive's sharpest **theoretical** framing of privacy and AI. His recurring move is to
reject the dominant consent/notice paradigm — "What is Privacy? That's the Wrong
Question" — and to argue for structural limits on data power. From 2024 his focus turns
to AI's effect on institutions ("Two AI Truths and a Lie," "How AI Destroys
Institutions"), bridging [[privacy-and-surveillance]] and
[[ai-governance-and-regulation]]. Though few in number, his items are all substantive
scholarship (mostly SSRN), several routed through the BKC Collection.
