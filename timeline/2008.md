# 2008

A US **election year**, read through the lens of a young field asking what networked
technology does to democracy. The Center's project portfolio is at its most expansive.

## The year's threads
- **The internet and democracy.** The **Internet & Democracy** project and **Publius**
  frame the election-year question of how the network reshapes political participation —
  the deep root of [[ai-and-democracy|today's democracy thread]].
- **Youth comes onto the agenda.** **Digital Natives** launches, opening BKC's long line
  of [[ai-chatbots-and-mental-health|youth-and-media]] work.
- **Global expression.** [[global-voices|Global Voices]], the **OpenNet Initiative**, and
  **Iran** keep [[digital-colonialism-and-global-south|global internet freedom]] central.
- **Generativity arrives.** [[jonathan-zittrain]] emerges as a defining voice with
  *The Future of the Internet — And How to Stop It*.

## Notable voices
Doc Searls, [[david-weinberger]], [[john-palfrey]], Wendy Seltzer, Charles Nesson, and
[[jonathan-zittrain]]. See [[berkman-klein-center]].
