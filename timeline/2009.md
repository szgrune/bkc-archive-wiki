# 2009

The year of **Iran's Green Movement** — the "Twitter Revolution" — when social media and
street protest fused and BKC's global-expression research found its defining case.

## The year's threads
- **Social media meets protest.** Iran's post-election uprising dominates, putting
  [[content-moderation-and-speech|platforms and political speech]] and
  [[digital-colonialism-and-global-south|global internet freedom]] at the center,
  tracked through [[global-voices|Global Voices]].
- **A new tool to watch censorship.** **Herdict** launches — crowdsourced reporting of
  what's blocked where — alongside the **OpenNet Initiative** and **Internet & Democracy**.
- **Security and trust online.** **StopBadware** and malware/transparency work continue.

## Notable voices
[[david-weinberger]], Doc Searls, Dan Gillmor, [[jonathan-zittrain]], Wendy Seltzer, and
Harry Lewis. See [[berkman-klein-center]].
