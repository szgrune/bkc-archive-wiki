# 2013

**Snowden and the surveillance turn** — the year the
[[privacy-and-surveillance|NSA disclosures]] reframed the whole field — bookended by the
death of Aaron Swartz, the open-access movement's martyr.

## The year's threads
- **After Snowden.** "Online security in the face of NSA surveillance" and urging tech
  companies to "push back against the NSA" — the start of [[cybersecurity-and-encryption]]
  as a mass-political concern.
- **Open access, won and mourned.** A new **White House open-access mandate** and
  **CopyrightX** launch; the community mourns **Aaron Swartz**. See
  [[copyright-and-open-access]].
- **Knowledge institutions.** The **Digital Public Library of America** launches
  ([[john-palfrey]]).
- **Teens, privacy, and civics.** New survey work on teens and privacy ([[danah-boyd]]);
  "generativity and originality" ([[jonathan-zittrain]]).
