# 2025

737 items — the prototype slice for this wiki. 2025 reads as the year the
**techlash matured into a structural-power critique** and the year the **AI build-out
hit the physical and human world**. Across sources, the dominant lens shifted from
"is AI good or bad?" to "who is accumulating power, at whose expense?"

## The year's threads
- **A regulatory tug-of-war.** California's [[ai-governance-and-regulation|SB 53]]
  passed while Congress fought over a federal moratorium pre-empting state law, against
  a deregulatory Trump administration and a diverging EU. See
  [[ai-governance-and-regulation]].
- **Surveillance came home.** [[surveillance-and-immigration-tech|ICE]] became the
  recurring protagonist of a domestic surveillance build-out — facial recognition, bulk
  location tracking, data fusion — documented largely by [[404-media]].
- **The platform-power reckoning.** [[platform-power-and-antitrust|Meta won its
  monopoly trial]] and the US-v-Google remedy debate sharpened, while [[tim-wu]]'s
  *Age of Extraction* gave the year its connective thesis.
- **AI's physical and human costs.** Data-center [[ai-and-the-environment|water and
  energy]] conflicts, the [[ai-labor-and-economy|jobs and "bubble"]] anxieties, and the
  [[digital-colonialism-and-global-south|hidden global-majority labor]] behind the
  models.
- **AI met the human psyche.** [[ai-chatbots-and-mental-health|Chatbots as therapists
  and companions]] — and the harms — became a front-page and regulatory concern.
- **Speech under pressure.** The [[content-moderation-and-speech|Supreme Court's TikTok
  ruling]], Meta's retreat from fact-checking, and visa policy weaponized against
  moderation work.

## Notable BKC moments
The Cyberlaw Clinic litigated social-media-surveillance and election-deepfake cases;
[[jonathan-zittrain]] spoke on AI agents and trust; [[bruce-schneier]] argued the
constructive case for AI in democracy. See [[berkman-klein-center]].
