# 2006

The archive opens here, with the first surviving **Berkman Buzz** newsletters (24 issues
from a half-year). The Center's early agenda is the **open web and citizen voice**: the
explosion of blogging and participatory journalism, a live fight over
[[copyright-and-open-access|copyright, DRM and the DMCA]], and the first
[[cybersecurity-and-encryption|anonymity and anti-malware]] tooling.

## The year's threads
- **The blogosphere as the main event.** Blogging, podcasting, and citizen media are the
  era's defining subject — the seedbed of [[journalism-and-the-news-business]] and of
  [[global-voices|Global Voices]], then nascent.
- **Open culture vs. control.** [[copyright-and-open-access|Copyright, DRM and the DMCA]]
  and early [[creative-commons|Creative Commons]] work, in [[lawrence-lessig]]'s orbit.
- **Circumvention and safety.** **Tor** anonymity and **StopBadware** (malware
  transparency) appear as recurring Center projects; early **China** censorship research.

## Notable voices
[[david-weinberger]], [[john-palfrey]], Dan Gillmor, Rebecca MacKinnon, [[lawrence-lessig]],
and Doc Searls recur across the year's issues. See [[berkman-klein-center]].
