# 2010

The year **state power and the open internet collide in public** — WikiLeaks and the
Google–China confrontation — while a Facebook privacy backlash marks the platform era's
arrival.

## The year's threads
- **WikiLeaks and the limits of openness.** The leaks and the response make
  [[cybersecurity-and-encryption|information control and security]] a front-page story.
- **Google vs. China.** The hacking-and-censorship standoff crystallizes
  [[digital-colonialism-and-global-south|state filtering vs. the global internet]],
  mapped by the **OpenNet Initiative** and **Herdict**.
- **Platform privacy backlash.** Rising [[privacy-and-surveillance|privacy]] alarm as
  Facebook's reach grows.
- **Citizen media institutionalizes.** **Radio Berkman**, Global Voices, and the
  **Citizen Media Law Project** carry the [[journalism-and-the-news-business|news]] thread.
