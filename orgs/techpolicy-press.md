# Tech Policy Press

The single most-cited dedicated outlet in the archive (142 items across the corpus; see
[[_domains|Sources by domain]]) and the curators' apparent house publication for
critical tech-policy analysis. It supplies the **power-focused, structural** framing
that recurs across nearly every topic here — surveillance, content moderation,
democracy, antitrust, digital colonialism. Its run reaches back to 2021 (NYPD
surveillance disclosures, the CFAA, "digital crime scenes" policing LGBTQ communities)
and only intensifies through the AI years. When an item interprets a development in
terms of *who gains power*, it is frequently from Tech Policy Press.
