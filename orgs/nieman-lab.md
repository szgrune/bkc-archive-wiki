# Nieman Journalism Lab

A project of the Nieman Foundation for Journalism at Harvard, and the archive's
**single most consistent source on the news business** — appearing nearly every year,
with a pronounced December spike from its annual **"Predictions for Journalism"**
series. Nieman supplies the archive's running commentary on the press's economics and
ethics: the limits of fact-checking, audience strategy, "dark patterns" in publishing,
the warning that "media owners will protect the powerful," and algorithm literacy as a
defense against misinformation. The empirical spine of
[[journalism-and-the-news-business]]. (A Harvard project, but distinct from the
[[berkman-klein-center]].)
