# Privacy at the Margins: IJOC Special Issue Introduction

danah boyd (Microsoft Research / Data & Society) and Alice Marwick co-authored the introduction to a special issue of the International Journal of Communication (IJOC) titled "Privacy at the Margins," arguing that privacy and surveillance operate differently for people at the margins of society—youth, immigrants, low-income communities, and others whose digital lives carry compounded vulnerabilities—than dominant policy frameworks assume. The piece challenges the convention of treating privacy as a uniform right experienced identically across all populations, and reframes surveillance scholarship around the specific structural conditions that shape exposure and harm for marginalised groups. The BKC orbit feed linked to the IJOC article on June 17, 2014; that same week the Berkman Buzz featured danah boyd writing on teen identity and commercial culture online, reflecting the coherent research agenda she was advancing at BKC during this period.
