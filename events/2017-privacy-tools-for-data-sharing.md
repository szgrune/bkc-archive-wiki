# Privacy Tools for Data Sharing: Lessons Learned and Directions Forward

A December 2017 symposium of Harvard's **Privacy Tools Project** — the multi-year,
cross-disciplinary effort (computer science, law, and social science) to build practical
mechanisms, including **differential privacy**, for sharing sensitive research data
without exposing individuals. The archive captures the occurrence through two distinct
artifacts: the project's own event page (the announcement) and the Eventbrite
registration. It sits at the applied, infrastructure end of [[privacy-and-surveillance]]
— the question of *how*, technically, to honor privacy while keeping data useful, the
same tension the [[privacy-and-surveillance|census differential-privacy debate]] would
later surface in public.
