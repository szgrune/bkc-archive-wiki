by the Berkman Center in collaboration with UNICEF that brings together 
a network of people from around the world who, together, are addressing 
the challenges and opportunities children and youth encounter in the 
digital environment.

    Zeynep Tufecki explains Turkey's Twitter block strategy
    [https://medium.com/message/everyone-is-getting-turkeys-twitter-block-wrong-cb596ce5f27](https://medium.com/message/everyone-is-getting-turkeys-twitter-block-wrong-cb596ce5f27)

Quote 	[https://cyber.law.harvard.edu/people/zeynep](https://cyber.law.harvard.edu/people/zeynep)

This is what Erdogan is now doing to social media: portray it as a place 
from which only ugly things come, and which poses a danger to family and 
to unity. Given that Turkey has a civil war that has erupted on its 
border with Syria, and is housing millions of distraught refugees, it is 
not hard to understand why people fear anything that they see as 
fomenting "disunity."

Erdogan is not trying to block social media as much as taint it.

 From her post on Medium, "Everyone Is Getting Turkey's Twitter Block 
Wrong"

The fierce backlash against Indiana's new law underscores how national

other tech firms have voiced their strong opposition to Indiana's new 
law, and others like it that are proliferating around the country. It's

    Susan Crawford suggests a different reason for Hillary's private
    email server
    [https://medium.com/backchannel/the-real-reason-for-hillary-s-private-email-server-dd6e19c8ca48](https://medium.com/backchannel/the-real-reason-for-hillary-s-private-email-server-dd6e19c8ca48)

Quote 	[https://cyber.law.harvard.edu/people/scrawford](https://cyber.law.harvard.edu/people/scrawford)

Former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton is nothing if not earnest. 
 From her high school time as a Goldwater Girl - "right down to my 
cowgirl outfit and straw cowboy hat emblazoned with the slogan 'AuH20'" 
- through her famously hard-working tenure as a cabinet official who 
traveled nearly a million miles and visited 112 countries during her 
long and exhausting stint in office, she has always Done Her Best. So 
why wouldn't Secy. Clinton have followed State Department policy warning 
against routine use of personal email accounts for government work?

Here's a possible reason: She wanted to be able to remember what she had 
done.

    Kate Darling makes a case against Google's new robot patent
    [http://spectrum.ieee.org/automaton/robotics/robotics-software/why-googles-robot-personality-patent-is-not-good-for-robotics](http://spectrum.ieee.org/automaton/robotics/robotics-software/why-googles-robot-personality-patent-is-not-good-for-robotics)

Quote 	[https://cyber.law.harvard.edu/people/kdarling](https://cyber.law.harvard.edu/people/kdarling)

In one example from the patent, the robot goes so far as to assemble an 
entire personality on request. "Be mom" could prompt it to search your 
phone and computer for information about "mom," determining her voice 
from recordings and displaying her photograph. Way to make cool 
technology sound creepy, Google. And it gets even better: At a symposium 
last Thursday, Sebastian Thrun, founder of the Google X laboratory, 
speculated "perhaps we can get to the point where we can outsource our 
own personal experiences entirely into a computer - and possibly our own 
personality."

 From her article in IEEE Spectrum, "Why Google's Robot Personality
