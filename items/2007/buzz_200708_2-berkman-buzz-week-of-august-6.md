/*The full buzz. */

"This week is the annual meeting of the Association for Education in 
Journalism and Mass Communication, better known in the field as AEJMC, 
where journalism and communications educators gather to ponder their 
profession. This will be my fourth such event, and in just a few years 
citizen journalism has moved from heresy -- a topic to be considered, if 
at all, only in side conferences and hallways -- to something that, 
while still not widely accepted, is at least of interest."
Excerpted from Dan Gillmor's blogpost, "Updating Journalism Education 
for This Century"
[http://citmedia.org/blog/2007/08/09/updating-journalism-education-for-this-century/](http://citmedia.org/blog/2007/08/09/updating-journalism-education-for-this-century/) 

About Dan Gillmor:
[http://www.dangillmor.com/about.htm](http://www.dangillmor.com/about.htm)

"Travels in June to Tanzania and South Africa - and specifically to the 
TED Global conference in Arusha, and a World Economic Forum meeting in 
Cape Town - have had me thinking more directly about economic 
development than I have for the past few years. Specifically, I came out 
of these conferences amazed by the success of the mobile phone industry 
in Africa and wondering whether what lessons other sectors could take 
from the mobile phone industry."
Excerpted from Ethan Zuckerman's blogpost, "Incremental Ideas"
[http://www.ethanzuckerman.com/blog/2007/08/05/incremental-ideas/](http://www.ethanzuckerman.com/blog/2007/08/05/incremental-ideas/)
About Ethan Zuckerman:
[http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/home/ethan_zuckerman](http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/home/ethan_zuckerman)

"A few days ago, I returned from Cambridge, MA, where I had been 
spending two inspiring and inspired weeks. Here are only three of the 
many (professional) highlights: I was very fortunate, once more, to be 
on the faculty of the OII summer doctoral program (SDP) that - for the 
first time - took place at Harvard Law School (and, no, there hasn't 
been an attempt to bring it to St. Gallen, rumors to the contrary...). 
My friends at the Berkman Center did a fantastic job in pulling together 
a very interesting summer program with a terrific line-up of contributors."
Excerpted from Urs Gasser's blogpost, "Cambridge, Summer 2007: A Few 
Impressions and a Thank You"
[http://blogs.law.harvard.edu/ugasser/2007/08/05/cambridge-summer-2007-a-few-impressions-and-a-thank-you/](http://blogs.law.harvard.edu/ugasser/2007/08/05/cambridge-summer-2007-a-few-impressions-and-a-thank-you/) 

About Urs Gasser:
[http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/home/urs_gasser](http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/home/urs_gasser)

"Wikipedia founder Jimmy 'Jimbo' Wales says that Wikia, his new 
for-profit wiki and search engine company, will never censor its content 
or compromise its users the way Google, Yahoo, Microsoft and others have 
done in China. In Taipei at Wikimania, the annual world conference for 
people who work on Wikipedia and all its associated projects, Jimbo gave 
a presentation about Wikia, describing how he plans to create an open 
search engine which anybody on the planet can help build, and whose 
algorithms will be made public."
Excerpted from Rebecca MacKinnon's blogpost, "Jimbo Wales: Google's 
China Mistake"
[http://rconversation.blogs.com/rconversation/2007/08/jimmy-wales-goo.html](http://rconversation.blogs.com/rconversation/2007/08/jimmy-wales-goo.html)
About Rebecca MacKinnon:
[http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/home/rebecca_mackinnon](http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/home/rebecca_mackinnon)

"Living in coastal California can dull one's Eastern edge, forged in the 
heat of summer, sharpened by abrasive seasons, the recurrent swelters 
and chills of true summers and winters. I've always been, as my old 
business partner David Hodskins correctly put it, comfort-imperative. 
Maybe that's one reason I stayed so long on the California coast after 
David took our company there from North Carolina for good business 
reasons: there was only one Silicon Valley, and that's where we 
belonged. Temperate conditions certainly helped draw me to Santa 
Barbara, although I would have gone and stayed anywhere my wife liked."
Excerpted from Doc Searls' blogpost, "Back to Natures"
[http://blogs.law.harvard.edu/doc/2007/08/07/back-to-natures/](http://blogs.law.harvard.edu/doc/2007/08/07/back-to-natures/)
About Doc Searls:
[http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/home/doc_searls](http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/home/doc_searls)

"I live in the Twin Cities, and the Law School where I teach is just a 
few blocks from the I-35W bridge that collapsed into the Mississippi 
River last week. (It's so nearby, in fact, that some of the 
investigators are using the school building as a temporary 
headquarters). I am fine and so is everyone I know. I was touched by the 
e-mails and phone calls my wife and I got right after the event from 
friends all around the country, checking in to make sure we all are 
safe. Some came from people I've just met a couple of times at 
conferences, but they were genuine and concerned. Others were from 
people who have been among my closest friends."
Excerpted from William McGeveran's blogpost, "Maintaining our Personal 
Bridges"
[http://blogs.law.harvard.edu/infolaw/2007/08/07/maintaining-our-personal-bridges/](http://blogs.law.harvard.edu/infolaw/2007/08/07/maintaining-our-personal-bridges/) 

About William McGeveran:
[http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/home/william_mcgeveran](http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/home/william_mcgeveran)

"Last Friday night (8/3), the United States Senate passed the the FOIA 
reform bill, S.849, before retiring for its August recess. We discussed 
the proposed FOIA amendments in detail a few weeks ago, when Senator Kyl 
was still holding the bill up in the Senate. The most notable aspect of 
the draft legislation from our perspective is its expansive definition 
of "the news media," which appears to encompass bloggers and other 
online journalists."
Excerpted from Sam Bayard's blogpost, "U.S. Senate Approves FOIA 
Amendments"
[http://www.citmedialaw.org/u-s-senate-approves-foia-amendments](http://www.citmedialaw.org/u-s-senate-approves-foia-amendments)
About Citizen Media Law Project:
[http://www.citmedialaw.org/about](http://www.citmedialaw.org/about)

"Morocco has made headlines all too often this past year for free speech 
issues. In December, there was the banning of Morocco's only darija 
(Moroccan dialect) magazine, Nichane, Aboubakr Jamai was practically 
forced out of the country to save his magazine, Le Journal, from a hefty 
fine, May saw Morocco named to CPJ's Backsliders list of countries where 
press freedom has most deteriorated in recent years, and now, to top it 
all off, a new story unfolds."
Excerpted from Jillian York's blogpost, "Morocco: Censorship Makes 
Headlines Yet Again"
[http://www.globalvoicesonline.org/2007/08/09/morocco-censorship-makes-headlines-yet-again/](http://www.globalvoicesonline.org/2007/08/09/morocco-censorship-makes-headlines-yet-again/)
