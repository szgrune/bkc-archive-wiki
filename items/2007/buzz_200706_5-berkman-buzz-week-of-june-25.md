/*The full buzz.*/

"Today thousands of Internet radio stations are observing a Day of 
Silence to bring attention to the death sentence they face when new 
royalty rates come into effect on July 16.  The day is organized by Kurt 
Hanson of RAIN (Radio And Internet Newsletter) and led by KCRW in Los 
Angeles, which is running interviews with Internet radio notables 
(including Kurt) throughout the day."
Excerpted from Doc Searl's blogpost, "Quiet case"
[http://doc.weblogs.com/2007/06/26#quietCase](http://doc.weblogs.com/2007/06/26#quietCase)
About Doc Searls:
[http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/home/doc_searls](http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/home/doc_searls)

"In working at the intersection of the Internet and international 
affairs, I meet a lot of people who believe that the connections we're 
able to build with one another in this new virtual space will lead 
towards a more inclusive political future. My friend and colleague Jim 
Moore articulated an especially strong version of this argument in his 
essay The Second Superpower Rears its Beautiful Head, but you can find 
versions of the argument in the work of many people I admire, including 
John Perry Barlow, Larry Lessig and Yochai Benkler."
Excerpted from Ethan Zuckerman's blogpost, "Fred Turner: the rhetoric of 
cyberutopianism"
[http://www.ethanzuckerman.com/blog/2007/06/26/fred-turner-the-rhetoric-of-cyberutopianism/](http://www.ethanzuckerman.com/blog/2007/06/26/fred-turner-the-rhetoric-of-cyberutopianism/)
About Ethan Zuckerman:
[http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/home/ethan_zuckerman](http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/home/ethan_zuckerman)

"As I've mentioned on this blog before, over the past year I've been 
involved with a process to establish a set of global principles on free 
speech and privacy protection for internet and telecoms companies. In 
January, Google, Yahoo!, Microsoft and Vodaphone publicly admitted to 
being part of this process. I recently wrote a brief article discussing 
the reasons why I think the process is a good thing." 
Excerpted from Rebecca MacKinnon's blogpost, "Free speech, privacy, and 
corporate responsibility: an update"
[http://feeds.feedburner.com/~r/blogs/RConversation/~3/127945405/a-global-commit.html](http://feeds.feedburner.com/~r/blogs/RConversation/~3/127945405/a-global-commit.html)
About Rebecca MacKinnon:
[http://rconversation.blogs.com/about.html](http://rconversation.blogs.com/about.html)

"ICANN's traveling circus meets in San Juan, Puerto Rico this week. One 
of the main subjects of discussion has been the introduction of new 
generic Top-Level Domains (gTLDs), after a GNSO Report proposed 19 
"Recommendations" for criteria these new domain strings should meet -- 
including morality tests and 'infringement' oppositions."
Excerpted from Wendy Seltzer's blogpost, "ICANN: Keep the Core Neutral, 
Stupid"
[http://wendy.seltzer.org/blog/archives/2007/06/25/icann_keep_the_core_neutral_stupid.html](http://wendy.seltzer.org/blog/archives/2007/06/25/icann_keep_the_core_neutral_stupid.html)
About Wendy Seltzer:
[http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/seltzer.html](http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/seltzer.html)

"The Supreme Court has once again cut away at student speech rights. In 
its recent decision Morse v. Frederick, the Court held that a high 
school did not violate the First Amendment when it suspended a student 
for displaying a 14-foot banner proclaiming 'Bong Hits for Jesus' during 
an Olympic torch parade."
Excerpted from Mary-Rose Papandrea's blogpost, "U.S. Supreme Court 
Limits Student Speech Rights"
[http://www.citmedialaw.org/u-s-supreme-court-limits-student-speech-rights](http://www.citmedialaw.org/u-s-supreme-court-limits-student-speech-rights)
About Citizen Media Law Project:
[http://www.citmedialaw.org/about](http://www.citmedialaw.org/about)

"From his prison cell in Bizerte (65km north of Tunis), The Tunisian 
prisoner Ramzi Bettibi managed to smuggle a very alarming letter that 
found its way on to the Internet (available in French). Ramzi is serving 
a four-year sentence at Bizerte for copying, onto a forum board he 
moderated, an online statement from a group threatening terror attacks 
if former Israeli prime minister Ariel Sharon attended the World Summit 
on the Information Society (WSIS) that was held in Tunisia in 2005."
Excerpted from Sami Ben Gharbia's blogpost, "CIA facility in Tunisia?"
[http://www.globalvoicesonline.org/2007/06/26/cia-facility-in-tunisia/](http://www.globalvoicesonline.org/2007/06/26/cia-facility-in-tunisia/)
