The full buzz.

“While digital signatures are widely used for verification, the same 
signature on each item is a privacy-busting linkage. With the help of 
third parties and selective disclosure proofs, however, we can make 
assertions that are minimal and don't leave a trail. We can create 
digital one-time cards each time we're asked for a facet of our identities.”
Excerpted from Wendy Seltzer’s blogpost, “Selective Disclosure and Privacy”
[http://wendy.seltzer.org/blog/archives/2007/05/02/selective_disclosure_and_privacy.html](http://wendy.seltzer.org/blog/archives/2007/05/02/selective_disclosure_and_privacy.html)
About Wendy Seltzer:
[http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/seltzer.html](http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/seltzer.html)

“If you missed the live program, as I did, you can watch ‘Bill Moyers 
Journal: Buying the War’ -- a brilliant documentary that everyone who 
cares about the future of American journalism should see. The report 
‘examines the press coverage in the lead-up to the war as evidence of a 
paradigm shift in the role of journalists in democracy and asks, four 
years after the invasion, what’s changed?’”
Excerpted from Dan Gillmor’s Center for Citizen Media blogpost, “How 
Press Failed on Iraq”
[http://citmedia.org/blog/2007/04/29/how-press-failed-on-iraq/](http://citmedia.org/blog/2007/04/29/how-press-failed-on-iraq/)
About Dan Gillmor:
[http://www.dangillmor.com/about.htm](http://www.dangillmor.com/about.htm)

“Snarkier-than-thou Gawker focused its lazy weekend eye on our very own 
Public Radio Talent Quest, kind of dissing it and loving it at the same 
time. My guess is they were going in for a short dig and then, like the 
rest of us, got drawn into the remarkably evolving and compelling world 
of this contest. Listening to random entries back-to-back is addictive 
enough, and now the message boards and comments are taking on a life of 
their own.”
Excerpted from Jake Shapiro’s blogpost, “Gross! Contest Attracts Public 
Radio Amateurs.”
[http://www.jakeshapiro.com/2007/04/30/gross-contest-attracts-public-radio-amateurs/](http://www.jakeshapiro.com/2007/04/30/gross-contest-attracts-public-radio-amateurs/)
About Jake Shapiro:
[http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/people/jshapiro.html](http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/people/jshapiro.html)

“Widespread use of Flickr to spread the URLs of unlocked proxy servers 
could have an effect in countries like China, UAE and Ethiopia… until 
governments began blocking Flickr (which they have in many countries 
that filter the Internet.) To spread clandestine information, it’s 
important not to have a single point of failure, like a single website 
that can be blocked. That, in turn, requires that an idea “go viral”, 
that hundreds of individuals decide to start spreading the information.”
Excerpted from Ethan Zuckerman’s blogpost, “Does the Number Have Lesson 
for Human Rights Activities?”
[http://www.ethanzuckerman.com/blog/?p=1410](http://www.ethanzuckerman.com/blog/?p=1410)
About Ethan Zuckerman:
[http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/home/ethan_zuckerman](http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/home/ethan_zuckerman)

“I met Valenti just about 10 years ago while I was at Harvard. Soon 
after I met him, he published a piece that was extremely nasty about me. 
A couple months later, he came to Harvard to debate me. He began by 
apologizing, marking the piece as the “dumbest” thing he had written. 
 From that moment on, every moment I was privileged to know the man was 
also a surprise. He was brilliant, and funny, and extraordinarily 
generous. We debated four times. In each he was self-deprecating, funny, 
and very very clever — feigning ignorance where it helped, pouncing when 
it worked.”
Excerpted from Lawrence Lessig’s blogpost, “Jack Valenti Dies”
[http://lessig.org/blog/archives/003757.shtml](http://lessig.org/blog/archives/003757.shtml)
About Lawrence Lessig:
[http://www.lessig.org/bio/short/](http://www.lessig.org/bio/short/)

"Tonight at 8pm Eastern (5pm Pacific), the PBS program Now will 
broadcast the story of Steven Vincent, the journalist whose blog, In the 
Red Zone, outlives him. Steven was murdered on August 2, 2005, three 
days after the New York Times published an op-ed piece by him. His wife 
Lisa later wrote, 'Steven ... has the dubious distinction of being one 
of the few foreign journalists in this Iraq conflict specifically 
targeted for execution.'"
