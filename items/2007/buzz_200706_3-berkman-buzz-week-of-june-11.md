"It's three days after the TED conference, and I've had two days to 
enjoy a rainy, but very beautiful, Cape Town and do some digesting of 
thoughts and ideas. (lots of pictures, for those interested...) I had 
breakfast this morning with Russell Southwood and our conversation 
reminded me just how exciting the conference had been, and how unexpected."
Excerpted from Ethan Zuckerman's blogpost, "Digesting TED Global - I'm

"Got a call from my mom today, she watched the Sopranos finale, like 
everyone. When the phone rang I fumbled with the Blackberry, hit a wrong 
key and disconnected the call. I tried calling back while she left a 
voicemail, and all the while I got more anxious that someone in the 
family had died or was horribly sick, and the more time went by the more 
sure I was and the more prepared I was for something horrible."
Excerpted from Dave Winer's blogpost, "The Sopranos aftermath"
[http://www.scripting.com/stories/2007/06/11/theSopranosAftermath.html](http://www.scripting.com/stories/2007/06/11/theSopranosAftermath.html)

"These are the kinds of stories that make me pull my hair out in despair 
at the way in which people toss around nonexistent intellectual property 
rights when they dislike someone else's portrayal of them. It shows why 
robust trademark fair use rights are so important (which, no 
coincidence, is the topic of my writing project this summer)."
Excerpted from William McGeveran's blogpost, "Trademark Fair Use and an 
Alien Gunfight in Manchester Cathedral"
[http://blogs.law.harvard.edu/infolaw/2007/06/11/trademark-fair-use-and-an-alien-gunfight-in-manchester-cathedral/](http://blogs.law.harvard.edu/infolaw/2007/06/11/trademark-fair-use-and-an-alien-gunfight-in-manchester-cathedral/)

"It's not just the pros who want control. Over the weekend the NCAA 
ejected a Louisville Courier-Journal reporter from a college baseball 
championship for live-blogging the game. Brian Bennet reports that he 
had been posting updates throughout the game on his Courier-Journal 
blog, until, at the bottom of the fifth inning, 'an NCAA representative 
came to my seat on press row and asked for my credential and asked me to 
leave. I complied.'"
Excerpted from Wendy Seltzer's blogpost, "NCAA Calls Foul on Reporter's 
Blogging"
[http://wendy.seltzer.org/blog/archives/2007/06/13/ncaa_calls_foul_on_reporters_blogging.html](http://wendy.seltzer.org/blog/archives/2007/06/13/ncaa_calls_foul_on_reporters_blogging.html)
About Wendy Seltzer:
[http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/seltzer.html](http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/seltzer.html)

"LA Times: AT&T to target pirated content: It joins Hollywood in trying 
to keep bootleg material off its network. Its network, the headline 
says. Not 'the Internet,' but its network.  If you had any illusions 
that what you get from the likes of AT&T is 'the Internet,' you've just 
been corrected.  Remember Ma Bell? Sheee's back! And now she's got the 
TV and 'the Internet' as well as the phone."
Excerpted from Doc Searls' blogpost, "Dear AT&T: Please go to hell"
[http://doc.weblogs.com/2007/06/13#dearAtamptPleaseGoToHell](http://doc.weblogs.com/2007/06/13#dearAtamptPleaseGoToHell)
About Doc Searls:

kept his promise to unblock the popular video-sharing site, YouTube. 
YouTube, which is owned by Google, was blocked by the Thai government in 
April 2007, following the appearance on the site of material critical of 
the country's king."
