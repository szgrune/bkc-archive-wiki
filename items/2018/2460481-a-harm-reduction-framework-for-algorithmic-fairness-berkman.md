# A Harm-Reduction Framework for Algorithmic Fairness | Berkman Klein Center

**Source:** [cyber.harvard.edu](http://cyber.harvard.edu/publication/2018/harm-reduction-framework-algorithmic-fairness) · **Published:** 2018-08-21 · **Feed tags:** buzz

> A Harm-Reduction Framework for Algorithmic Fairness
> 
> When algorithms are left to make important decisions, the results can have a profound impact on individuals, argue Berkman Klein Fellow [Alex Wood](http://cyber.harvard.edu/people/awood), Faculty Associate [Effy Vayena](http://cyber.harvard.edu/people/evayena) and [Micah Altman](http://cyber.harvard.edu/people/micah-altman), the Director of Research at MIT Libraries, in a [new report](http://cyber.harvard.edu/publication/2018/harm-reduction-framework-algorithmic-fairness) published by IEEE Security & Privacy. After studying foreseeable and unforeseeable harms, the authors propose a harm-reduction framework for making algorithms more fair.
