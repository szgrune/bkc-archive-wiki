# A Victory for Software Preservation: DMCA Exemption Granted for SPN | Cyberlaw Clinic

**Source:** [clinic.cyber.harvard.edu](https://clinic.cyber.harvard.edu/2018/10/26/a-victory-for-software-preservation-dmca-exemption-granted-for-spn/) · **Published:** 2018-10-26 · **Feed tags:** website, mailchimp, buzz

> /// Yesterday, the Library of Congress handed a significant win to digital preservationists. On October 26, 2018, the Library of Congress granted an exemption to the DMCA’s anti-circumvention provision for libraries, archives, and museums to circumvent technological protection measures on certain lawfully acquired software for the purposes of preserving software and materials that depend on it.///
> 
>  
> 
> A Victory for Software Preservation: DMCA Exemption Granted for SPN
> 
> The [HLS Cyberlaw Clinic](https://clinic.cyber.harvard.edu/), representing the Software Preservation Network, [received an exemption](https://clinic.cyber.harvard.edu/2018/10/26/a-victory-for-software-preservation-dmca-exemption-granted-for-spn/) to the Digital Millennium Copyright Act anti-circumvention provisions to allow libraries, archivists, museums, and other cultural heritage institutions to preserve software and software-dependent materials. [Motherboard](https://motherboard.vice.com/en_us/article/zm9az5/copyright-law-just-got-better-for-video-game-history) also interviewed [Kendra Albert](https://cyber.harvard.edu/people/kalbert), a clinical instructional fellow at the Cyberlaw Clinic, about how this ruling benefits video game history.
