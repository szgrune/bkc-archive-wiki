# Restaurants have strict standards to protect customers. Tech platforms don't

**Source:** [cbc.ca](https://www.cbc.ca/radio/spark/411-1.4879512/restaurants-have-strict-standards-to-protect-customers-tech-platforms-don-t-1.4879520) · **Published:** 2018-10-30 · **Feed tags:** website, mailchimp, buzz

> Restaurants have strict standards to protect customers. Tech platforms don't
> 
> Online tech platforms should become “information fiduciaries,” argues Berkman Klein co-founder [Jonathan Zittrain](https://cyber.harvard.edu/people/jzittrain) in this [CBC radio interview](https://www.cbc.ca/radio/spark/411-1.4879512/restaurants-have-strict-standards-to-protect-customers-tech-platforms-don-t-1.4879520). "The basic idea behind the fiduciary relationship is that the way they use that sensitive data should be to advance our interests and where their interests happen to conflict with ours, they've got to favor ours over theirs," he says.

> //One of the reasons you're able to enjoy a meal in a restaurant is because you're not too worried that it's clean in the kitchen. 
> 
> That's because you know that restaurants have to meet a minimum standard of cleanliness, or risk being shut down. The restaurant is obligated to act in the best interests of its diners. 
> 
> This is one example used by [Jonathan Zittrain](https://hls.harvard.edu/faculty/directory/10992/Zittrain) to argue that it may be time to create a similar kind of obligation for social media platforms and other tech giants who hold our personal data.///
