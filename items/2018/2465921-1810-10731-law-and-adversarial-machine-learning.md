# [1810.10731] Law and Adversarial Machine Learning

**Source:** [arxiv.org](https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.10731) · **Published:** 2018-10-26 · **Feed tags:** website, mailchimp, buzz

> //Lots of Berkfolk: [Ram Shankar Siva Kumar](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Kumar%2C+R+S+S), [David R. O'Brien](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=O%27Brien%2C+D+R), [Kendra Albert](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Albert%2C+K), [Salome Vilojen](https://arxiv.org/search/cs?searchtype=author&query=Vilojen%2C+S) When machine learning systems fail because of adversarial manipulation, what kind of legal relief can society expect? Through scenarios grounded in adversarial ML literature, we explore how some aspects of computer crime, copyright, and tort law interface with perturbation, poisoning and model stealing, model inversion attacks to show how some attacks are more likely to result in liability than others. We end with a call for action to ML researchers to invest in transparent benchmarks of attacks and defenses; architect ML systems with forensics in mind and finally, think more about adversarial machine learning in the context of civil liberties. The paper is targeted towards ML researchers who have no legal background.///
