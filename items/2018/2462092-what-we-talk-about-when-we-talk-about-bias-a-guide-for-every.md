> What We Talk About When We Talk About Bias (A guide for everyone)
> 
> “Ultimately, the goal of researchers, developers, and regulators should be to detect and combat bias along multiple stages of the development pipeline,” writes Ayesha Bajwa, an alumna of [Jonathan Zittrain](http://cyber.harvard.edu/people/jzittrain) and [Joi Ito](http://cyber.harvard.edu/people/jito)’s joint course on [Ethics and Governance of Artificial Intelligence](https://www.media.mit.edu/courses/the-ethics-and-governance-of-artificial-intelligence/), in [this Medium post](https://medium.com/@ayesharbajwa/what-we-talk-about-when-we-talk-about-bias-a-guide-for-everyone-3af55b85dcdc). The paper, based on a paper written for the course, outlines multiple perspectives of machine learning bias and couples them with current examples.
