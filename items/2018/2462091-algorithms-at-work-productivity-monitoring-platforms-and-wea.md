> Algorithms at Work: Productivity Monitoring Platforms and Wearable Technology as the New Data-Centric Research Agenda for Employment and Labor Law

> “The 21st century has ushered in new technologies uniquely designed to attend to employers’ interests in profit-maximization but those new technologies also bring new concerns regarding employee privacy and employment discrimination,” writes Berkman Klein Faculty Associate [Ifeoma Ajunwa](https://cyber.harvard.edu/people/iajunwa) in [this essay](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3247286) on the use of algorithms to monitor employees, and the legal implications of this practice.
