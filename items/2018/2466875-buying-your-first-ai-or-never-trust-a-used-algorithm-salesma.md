# Buying your First AI or “Never Trust a Used Algorithm Salesman”

**Source:** [medium.com](https://medium.com/berkman-klein-center/buying-your-first-ai-136cd2e6dd2) · **Published:** 2018-11-07 · **Feed tags:** mailchimp, website, buzz

> "There was a time when more of us knew how to change the oil and change a timing belt, keep an engine running and fix the brakes. Cars got more complicated and we got less mechanical, but one thing hasn’t changed: if you are buying a used car, you can’t trust the seller to tell you what’s wrong with it," writes BKC affiliate [Gretchen Greene](https://cyber.harvard.edu/people/gretchen-greene) in [this Medium post](https://medium.com/berkman-klein-center/buying-your-first-ai-136cd2e6dd2). "If you’re a government official buying an algorithm, whether marketed as predictive analytics, AI, or machine learning, the same thing is true. It’s great if you have a mechanic you trust. It’s even better if you also have some idea of what to look for yourself."
