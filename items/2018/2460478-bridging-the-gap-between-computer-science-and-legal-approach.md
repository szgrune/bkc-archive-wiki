# Bridging the Gap between Computer Science and Legal Approaches to Privacy | Berkman Klein Center

**Source:** [cyber.harvard.edu](http://cyber.harvard.edu/publication/2018/bridging-gap-between-computer-science-and-legal-approaches-privacy) · **Published:** 2018-08-21 · **Feed tags:** buzz

> Bridging the Gap between Computer Science and Legal Approaches to Privacy
> 
> Recent data breaches have shown that data privacy is an increasing concern. A [new article](http://cyber.harvard.edu/publication/2018/bridging-gap-between-computer-science-and-legal-approaches-privacy) from Berkman Klein Executive Director [Urs Gasser](http://cyber.harvard.edu/people/ugasser), Fellow [Alex Wood](http://cyber.harvard.edu/people/awood), and Senior Researcher [David O’Brien](http://cyber.harvard.edu/people/dobrien), among others, proposes a methodology for thinking about privacy through models from both computer science models and the law. The article was published in the Spring 2018 Edition of [Harvard Journal of Law & Technology](https://jolt.law.harvard.edu/assets/articlePDFs/v31/02.-Article-Wood-7.21.pdf).
