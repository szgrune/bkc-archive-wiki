# Better Tech by Francine Berman: 9780262054881 | PenguinRandomHouse.com: Books

**Source:** [penguinrandomhouse.com](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/books/828092/better-tech-by-francine-berman/) · **Published:** 2026-06-09 · **Feed tags:** bkc-happenings, community

> Faculty Associate Fran Berman has a significant new book coming this fall. She writes, "I’m delighted to announce that my new book -- [Better Tech: Putting People First in Cyberspace](https://www.franberman.com/) -- will be published by MIT Press on September 15!  In a very real sense, this book was born from BKC and our BKC Public Interest Tech Working Group.  I am so grateful to you all for your wisdom, thought-provoking ideas, discussions, writings, and your passionate belief in the public good. Thank you all!
> 
> What’s the book about?  Better Tech is a practical guide to making the technologies we depend on safer, more trustworthy, and more beneficial for the people who use them. The book goes beyond identifying the problems of technology and provides a playbook of actionable strategies for responsible design, development, use, and governance that can be used by everyone, from policy makers to students and general readers.   If you are interested in the book for yourself, your organization, your class, or your library, it’s available now for pre-order and I’d be happy to work with you on an event for your group.
> 
> Much gratitude to Jill Lepore and our own Bruce Schneier for their generous endorsements, and to the many colleagues, collaborators, and communities who influenced the book, including Josh Greenberg and colleagues at the Sloan Foundation, fellow Fellows at the Radcliffe Institute, colleagues at the PIT University Network, UMass Amherst (and especially thePIT@UMass group), RPI, the San Diego Supercomputer Center, and especially the BKC Hivemind and BKCPIT Working Group (shout out to my co-chair Anne Washington).   
> 
> I’d love to see Berkmaniacs at any of the book events this fall and will share more information about the schedule in the coming months.  For now, I wanted to share this exciting news—and thank all of you who helped make the book possible!"
