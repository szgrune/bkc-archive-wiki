# Location, Location, Location. And the Virus.

**Source:** [lawfareblog.com](https://www.lawfareblog.com/location-location-location-and-virus) · **Published:** 2020-03-30 · **By:** Stewart Baker · **Feed tags:** addedcommunity, community

> [David Kris](https://www.twitter.com/davidkris), [Paul Rosenzweig](https://www.twitter.com/rosenzweigp) and I dive deep on the big tech issue of the COVID-19 contagion: Whether (but mostly how) to use mobile phone location services to fight the virus. We cover [the Israeli approach](https://www.wsj.com/articles/israel-turns-to-its-spy-agencies-to-combat-coronavirus-11584735025), as well as a host of solutions adopted in Singapore, [Taiwan, South Korea and elsewhere](https://medicalxpress.com/news/2020-03-countries-coronavirus.html). I’m a big fan of Singapore, which produced in a week [an app](https://www.straitstimes.com/sites/default/files/attachments/2020/03/21/vnapp_5541158.pdf) that [Nick Weaver](https://www.twitter.com/ncweaver) thought would take a year.
> 
> In our interview, [evelyn douek](https://www.evelyndouek.com), currently at the Berkman Klein Center and an SJD candidate at Harvard, takes us deep into [content moderation](https://www.lawfareblog.com/verified-accountability-self-regulation-content-moderation-answer-special-problems-speech-0). Displaying a talent for complexifying an issue we all want to simplify, she explains why we can’t get live with social platform censorship and why we can’t live without it. She walks us through the growth of content moderation, from spam, through child pornography and on to terrorism and “coordinated inauthentic behavior”—the identification of which, evelyn assures me, does not require an existentialist dance instructor. Instead, it’s the latest and least easily defined category of speech to be suppressed by Big Tech.
> 
> Returning to the News Roundup, [Nate Jones](https://www.twitter.com/n8jones81) and evelyn mull the [head-spinning change](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/03/23/technology/coronavirus-facebook-amazon-youtube.html?utm_campaign=The%20Interface&utm_medium=email&utm_source=Revue%20newsletter) the virus has made in the public reputation of Big Tech, but Nate wonders [if Silicon Valley's PR glow will last](https://www.politico.com/news/2020/03/25/silicon-valley-virus-era-dc-glow-147178).
> 
> Meanwhile, China is celebrating its self-proclaimed victory over COVID-19 by [borrowing Russian tactics](https://www.cyberscoop.com/coronavirus-disinformation-china-russia-cyber-peace-institute/) to [spread coronavirus disinformation](https://www.propublica.org/article/how-china-built-a-twitter-propaganda-machine-then-let-it-loose-on-coronavirus). I argue that any country adopting Russia’s patented “Who knows what’s true?” tactics probably has something to hide.
> 
> We take advantage of evelyn’s Aussie ties to get a translation (and an apology) for Australia’s latest venture into the business of [blocking graphic violent content](https://www.zdnet.com/article/isps-to-continue-blocking-graphic-violent-content-in-australia/).
> 
> David and Paul review the White House’s [National Strategy for 5G Security](https://www.whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2020/03/National-Strategy-5G-Final.pdf). They talk for two minutes, but they say more than the strategy.
> 
> The House of Representative has irresponsibly bolted for home [without even a temporary reauthorization](https://www.nytimes.com/2020/03/27/us/politics/house-fisa-bill.html) of expiring FISA authorities. Paul and David explain why that isn’t quite the disaster it sounds like. Quite.
> 
> David says the Justice Department has brought the [first fraud case stemming from the coronavirus crisis](https://www.washingtonpost.com/national-security/coronavirus-vaccine-fraud-case-justice-department/2020/03/22/3ac4014e-6c72-11ea-b148-e4ce3fbd85b5_story.html?utm_campaign=wp_the_cybersecurity_202&utm_medium=email&utm_source=newsletter&wpisrc=nl_cybersecurity202), and I suggest that case itself has a whiff of false advertising about it.
> 
> [Amazon is complaining](https://www.wsj.com/articles/amazon-claims-pentagons-jedi-overhaul-keeps-microsoft-in-drivers-seat-11585069521?mod=djemRiskCompliance) that the Pentagon is trying to fix some of the contract award problems in the big Defense Department cloud procurement. Paul is more sympathetic than I am.
> 
> And Paul questions the wisdom of [failing to delay CCPA enforcement](https://globaldatareview.com/data-privacy/covid-19-will-not-delay-ccpa-enforcement) while the coronavirus rages across California.
